About


This Index offers updated and updateable descriptions of the people, imprints, and journals that were a part of Virginia's print trade from its first offering in 1682 through those produced for the 1820 General Assembly. And as such it represents one approach to the future of scholarly reference works, particularly ones with a bibliographic or biographic focus.

This resource is comprised of three elements:
  • The first is a series of short biographies for each of the {{ num_bios }} individuals known to have worked in the trade, whether as tradesmen, editors, or publishers.

  • The second element offers a bibliographic record for each of the {{ num_imprints }} imprint documents produced in Virginia from 1730 to 1820, complete with cross-references to the standard bibliographies of early American imprints.

  • The third element is a collection of narrative histories for each of the {{ num_news_hists }} newspapers. that were started in Virginia before 1820, covering their evolution and various incarnations, so encompassing {{ num_news_cites }} individual titles.

    •  Using this site's search capabilities, this Index allows researchers to better situate all three components of the printing trade in their own investigations into Virginia's history.
